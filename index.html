ServiceLink Candidate vision for Senior Product Designer, AI - by Russell Dudek

Give every agent clear title to act.

Mortgage workflows cannot trade trust for speed. The product-design opportunity is to make every consequential AI action legible: what the agent understood, which evidence it used, what authority it held, where a human remains accountable, and how the system recovers when reality refuses to cooperate.

The layers align only when the product makes intent, evidence, authority and recovery coherent. Decorative AI magic is not invited to closing.

Why this role matters now

ServiceLink already connects the transaction. AI raises the standard for how clearly it explains itself.

Scale and consequence

ServiceLink describes an end-to-end mortgage platform spanning title, closing, valuation, flood, default and investor workflows, with 25 million-plus services delivered annually.

Borrower expectation

Its current research and product messaging emphasize speed, convenience, transparency and human guidance - not automation for automation's sake.

Product foundation

EXOS already digitizes scheduling, title, disclosure and decision-support work. Agentic experiences will increasingly cross channels, functions and authority boundaries.

Design mandate

The Senior Product Designer must turn technical capability into interactions that preserve trust through clarification, confirmation, exception handling, handoff and recovery.

Public context: ServiceLink homepage, About, Lenders, Title, Closing, Valuation and 2026 borrower-experience insights. Internal workflows are not assumed.

The design work lives in the tension, not in the chat bubble.

Operating model

Clear Title to Act

A design system for deciding when an AI agent may act, when it must assist, and when the workflow needs a person.

Outcome: Every important action leaves a usable trust receipt for the user, operator and organization.
01 - INTENT

Establish the job being done.

Clarify the user's goal, transaction state, urgency and missing context before the agent races toward a plausible but wrong answer.

02 - EVIDENCE

Show what supports the action.

Surface the material data, source freshness, confidence and contradiction that a person needs to understand the recommendation.

03 - AUTHORITY

Make decision rights explicit.

Distinguish what the agent may complete, draft, recommend, confirm, reverse, or never do without human approval.

04 - ENCUMBRANCE

Detect the condition that changes the rules.

Identify exceptions such as identity ambiguity, conflicting documents, regulated changes, borrower distress or unusual risk.

05 - RECOVERY

Design the path back to a good state.

Clarify, offer alternatives, preserve context, warm-transfer, reverse safely, explain what changed and resume without forcing the user to start over.

06 - RECEIPT

Leave the decision legible.

Record the action, evidence, authority, exception, human decision and next step in a form that supports auditability and learning.

Meaningful interaction

Run an Agent Title Review.

Choose a mortgage-workflow scenario. The model changes the action posture based on intent, evidence, authority, exception and recovery - not on a generic confidence score. The default is illustrative, not a claim about ServiceLink's internal process.

First 90 days

      
Learn the work. Make behavior visible. Prove one reusable pattern.

      

        
Days 1-30
Read the transaction
  • Map one priority workflow across user, operator, product, data, engineering and compliance perspectives.
  • Inventory agent behaviors, handoffs, exception classes and existing evidence.
  • Establish design principles and a shared vocabulary for authority and recovery.

        
Days 31-60
Prototype the control points
  • Prototype clarification, confirmation, escalation, recovery and trust-receipt patterns.
  • Test with frontline users and business stakeholders using realistic exceptions.
  • Create an evaluation rubric connecting usability, workflow outcomes, risk and adoption.

        
Days 61-90
Ship a reusable pattern
  • Partner with engineering and product to deliver one bounded workflow improvement.
  • Instrument the design with behavioral and operating signals.
  • Package the pattern, evidence and lessons for reuse across adjacent EXOS experiences.

“You have not held the conventional Senior Product Designer title in mortgage tech.”

      

        
The hard objection

        
True. The relevant question is whether the adjacent evidence is strong enough.

        
I would not pretend that operating leadership automatically equals years inside a formal design organization, or that adjacent financial and high-reliability work equals mortgage-domain tenure.

        
What transfers directly is the work underneath this role: mapping complex workflows, facilitating across technical and business teams, prototyping AI-assisted operating patterns, defining human authority and exceptions, creating decision artifacts, testing assumptions, and making consequential systems understandable to the people who use them.

        
The campaign is designed to let ServiceLink judge that work rather than accept a polished claim on faith.

      

    

  


  

    

      
Questions for the interview

      
The best design questions reveal where authority and accountability are already under strain.

      

        
Which mortgage workflows have the greatest combination of volume, user friction and exception cost - and which are intentionally not automation targets?

        
Where do ServiceLink users currently lose confidence in an automated recommendation: missing evidence, unclear status, poor recovery, or uncertainty about who owns the decision?

        
How are product, design, data science, engineering, operations, legal and compliance expected to resolve disagreements about agent authority?

        
What would count as a successful first AI design pattern: faster cycle time, fewer touches, higher completion, lower rework, stronger adoption, better trust signals, or a balanced combination?

        
How much of this role is designing customer-facing AI behavior versus improving the internal design-to-development operating system with AI tooling?
