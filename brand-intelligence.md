## Typography evidence

ServiceLink's public site uses a modern neo-grotesk sans-serif character: large high-weight headings, compact navigation, clean body copy, minimal decorative typography, and strong blue/white contrast. The precise licensed webfont could not be reliably retrieved through the connected source.

## Typography decision

Use `Aptos`, `Helvetica Neue`, `Arial`, sans-serif as a documented structural substitute. Fidelity is carried through weight, compact tracking, large headline scale, white space, and restrained UI typography. No proprietary font binaries are copied.

## Photography and illustration character

The official site uses people, borrower/mortgage-service situations, clean corporate imagery, large blue fields, and simple explanatory product content. This campaign avoids unlicensed corporate photography and instead creates an original role-derived system visualization.

## Geometry and interface motifs

- Bright blue bands and navy text.
- Strong rectangular sections, thin rules, generous whitespace.
- Clean product/service taxonomy.
- Low-radius controls and restrained shadows.
- Direct, benefit-oriented language around speed, transparency and borrower experience.

## Motion character

The official site is visually direct and editorial rather than motion-heavy. Candidate motion is therefore calm and explanatory: layered action-title documents align into a coherent decision state, and scenario controls update a bounded authority review.

## Overall vibe

Experienced, national-scale, trusted, service-led, technology-forward, borrower-centric and operationally precise.
