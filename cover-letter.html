Dear ServiceLink Hiring Team,

The hardest part of designing agentic mortgage experiences is not making an AI system feel intelligent. It is making the system's authority, evidence, uncertainty, handoff and recovery clear enough that borrowers, operators and institutions can trust what happens next.

That is why the Senior Product Designer, AI role caught my attention. ServiceLink already describes a technology-and-service model built around speed, transparency, borrower experience and the EXOS platform. The next design challenge is to preserve those strengths as AI agents move across workflows, channels and decision boundaries.

My career path is unconventional for a product-design role, and I will not disguise that. I have not spent years carrying a formal Senior Product Designer title in mortgage technology. I have spent years doing the adjacent work the role requires: mapping complex operations, facilitating across product, engineering, data and business stakeholders, designing AI-assisted workflows, prototyping human-in-the-loop patterns, defining escalation and recovery, building decision records, and making high-consequence systems visible enough to improve.

At Vape-Jet, I work directly across production, quality, customer support, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, Jira, telemetry and AI-assisted workflows. Through DudeWorth, I develop agentic patterns connecting structured context, retrieval, AI agents, human judgment, evaluation and governance. At Amazon and Compunetics, I built operating mechanisms, quality systems, escalation paths and customer-backward routines where ambiguity and weak handoffs carried real cost.

At ServiceLink, I would begin by tracing a small set of consequential workflows across the workflow interface, chat, SMS and voice: where an agent interprets intent, gathers evidence, recommends, acts, pauses, hands off or recovers. I would prototype the exception and recovery paths early, make human decision rights explicit, and align product, engineering, data science and business teams around a shared behavior model rather than a collection of disconnected screens.

This is not an argument for maximum autonomy. Mortgage workflows carry legitimate variation, compliance obligations, quality standards and moments when a borrower needs a capable human. The design goal is trustworthy acceleration: let AI do more only when its title to act is clear, and make every consequential action easy to inspect, challenge and improve.

I built an independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/servicelink/. It demonstrates a role-specific operating artifact called Clear Title to Act: a way to design every consequential agent action around visible intent, evidence, authority, exception conditions, recovery and a usable trust receipt. The site also includes an interactive Agent Title Review and a detailed first-90-day plan.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss where ServiceLink sees the greatest AI design tension today: autonomous speed versus human accountability, customized lender workflows versus consistent behavior, or multichannel convenience versus one coherent decision record.

Carpe diem,
Russell Dudek
